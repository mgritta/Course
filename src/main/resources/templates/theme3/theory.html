First, a bit of theory.

Russian verbs have many forms. The primary invariable verb form that usually appears in dictionaries is called the infinitive; in English the infinitve form of verbs always appears with preposition "to" such as "to read" or "to go".

The Russian infinitive ends either in -ть (читать - to read) or -ти (идти - to go). The infinitive of a small group of verbs take the ending –чь (беречь - to take care of). In accordance with their present tense forms, Russian verbs fall into two main groups or two conjugations.

The infinitive of most 1-st conjugation verbs ends in –ать, -ять, еть such as the verbs читать (to read), гулять (to walk), болеть ( to be ill with); уметь ( to can). The infinitive of most 2-nd conjugation verbs ends in –ить such as the verbs говорить (to speak), верить (to believe).

Infinitives ending in –еть, -ать may be either 1-st or 2-nd conjugation such as the verbs смотреть (to look), слышать ( to hear), видеть ( to see), дышать ( to breathe).

Infinitive endings in -ся/-сь are called reflexive verbs and may be either 1-st or 2-nd conjugation such as the verbs учить-ся, занимать-ся (учить-ся - to study).

Russian verbs have three tense: the present, the past and the future.

The present tense(настоящее) is used to denote:

  • An action taking place at the moment of speaking:
    • Сейчас мы занимаемся. We are studying.
  • An action taking place habitually:
    • Она изучает русский язык. She studies Russian.
  • An action which began in the past and is continuing at the moment of speaking:
    • Она изучает русский язык два года. She has been studying Russian for two years.

A verb in the present tense answers the questions:

  • Что я делаю? | What I am doing?
  • Что ты делаешь? | What do you do?/What are you doing?
  • Что он/она делает? | What does he/she do?/What is he/she doing?
